Life at IU Bloomington

IU Bloomington offers our biggest and most traditional campus life, with an incredible variety of academic and extracurricular opportunities, thriving campus traditions, and an unmistakable sense of Hoosier pride.

Picture yourself at IU Bloomington

It’s easy to fall in love with IU Bloomington’s beautiful campus, with iconic places like the picturesque Arboretum, the grand Musical Arts Center (“the MAC”), and the Indiana Memorial Union (IMU), one of the country’s largest student unions, with four floors of late-night dining options, study rooms, a bowling alley, a movie theater, and more.

Keep learning during challenging times

Head to our Keep Learning site to find technology resources, self care support, and opportunities to stay connected to the IU community during COVID-19, whether you’re on campus or learning remotely.

A lively campus residential life

About a third of undergraduates live on campus (including all first-year students), and IU Bloomington offers a range of different campus housing options, from traditional dorms to apartment-style housing.

Growing resources for culture and diversity

Culture and diversity are deep cornerstones of the IU Bloomington campus and college experience. Today, campus is home to six active centers and institutes for African-American Arts, Asian culture, Black culture, First Nations culture, Latino culture, and the LGBTQ+ community, as well as 200 student cultural organizations and counting.
